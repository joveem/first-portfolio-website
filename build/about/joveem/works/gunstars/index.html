Gameplay

In App Purchase validation

I implemented IAP purchase validation because it's relatively easy to bypass purchases on Android and iOS to simulate a fake purchase. This validation made it possible to identify a fake purchase, make it fail without having any effect and ensure that users do not get "infinite gems" or something like that

NFT Marketplace integration

I led a team of 3 programmers focused on implementing an integration with the Dropull marketplace to add a collection of collectible skins. I took the skins handling decisions on the front-end, implemented part of them and implemented everything on the back-end. These skins had some peculiarities compared to normal skins: they could be advertised on the marketplace and could be upgraded, which caused its asset to be destroyed and replaced with a higher level one.

Daily missions

Led a team of 2 programmers focused on implementing Daily Missions. I implemented the handling of quest progress from Gameplay events. I gave layout ideas to the art team to generate the visual assets. Defined and implemented the visual flow of UI animations and feedbacks. The backend was developed by another programmer based on my definitions

MatchMaking refactoring

I participated in a big change in match searches, the goal was to make players have a better experience by being able to see players entering the same match and solving several unexpected behaviors (such as "the player entered a different mode or map than he selected" and "a player was forever stuck in matchmaking"). I gave layout ideas to the art team to generate the visual assets. Defined and implemented the visual flow of UI animations and feedbacks. The backend was developed by another programmer based on old Matchmaking rules, new Game Designer requirements and some changes defined by me

Social/Party handling refactoring

I did a successful refactoring on the handling of friend states and team configuration in match search. The goal was to stop a lot of unexpected behavior, like "a player is ready but he shows up as not-ready to his friend", "a player is showing up in another's team when he shouldn't", and so on. The old implementation had an unnecessarily complex aprouch that made feature behavior unpredictable. Also implemented "match based friend suggestion" and "online friend alert"

Better log reading tool

I made a Python algorithm that made the text colors of the html log files (from TouchConsole Pro) be recovered, and changed the filtering mode, to make it highlight the logs instead of hiding them, and made that you could use more than one search term at the same time

Multiplayer gameplay bug fixes

Gameplay changes and bug fixes (like, "auto aim is shooting in the wrong place" and "weapons with area damage break give much more experience because they break several blocks of the scenery"). Initially, I joined to work only on that, but later I migrated to the back-end because of the needs of the project.
